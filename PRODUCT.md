# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Three overlapping groups arrive with a concrete problem and limited patience for sales theater:

1. Founders and early-stage teams who need senior technical judgment before (or instead of) hiring it full time: direction, architecture, a first dependable build, or an honest review of what they already have.
2. Companies and product leaders, often without a deep technical background, who need fragile delivery, unclear technical direction, media-heavy complexity, or a risky technology decision translated into plain consequences and a workable route forward.
3. Agencies and studios subcontracting specialist workstreams they cannot staff internally: multimedia systems, demanding software builds, architecture support, or an independent audit.

The strongest-fit visitor is not browsing for a generic agency. They have a specific problem and need to understand quickly whether Wojciech can inspect the situation, reduce ambiguity, and help move the work forward. Many of them are not engineers: the site must earn their trust in business terms first and technical evidence second.

## Product Purpose

The site presents Wojciech Bajer's one-person company as a selective consulting and delivery practice with four entry routes:

1. Independent audits and reviews: of a company, product, codebase, or technology decision, before cost and risk grow.
2. Hands-on delivery across multimedia systems, software engineering, and application architecture.
3. Ongoing fractional technical leadership: continuing direction and accountability without a full-time hire.
4. AI and LLM engineering: taking AI-dependent products and integrations from idea or prototype to something dependable.

Success means a qualified visitor quickly understands four things:

1. Which entry route matches their problem.
2. How the work moves from diagnosis to useful output.
3. Why direct one-person accountability is a trust signal, not a limitation.
4. How to route a concrete issue into contact without unnecessary friction.

The primary conversion path is direct email. The protected contact form exists as a secondary route, with email still visible when the form is loading or unavailable.

## Brand Personality

Technical, direct, approachable.

The brand should still feel like a focused operator at a live control surface, but one who welcomes the visitor in and speaks their language. Lead with the business outcome (risk reduced, delivery unblocked, decision de-risked, handoff durable), then show the technical evidence behind it. The voice is plain, specific, and calm: diagnostic without being cold, confident without sales theater. A non-technical founder should feel smarter after reading a page, not tested by it.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic agency polish, bloated SaaS landing-page patterns, vague innovation language, decorative case-study theater, and portfolio design that feels more interested in presentation than problem solving.

Do not make the site feel like a large anonymous consultancy. Do not bury the operator behind abstract company language. Do not turn technical identity into costume hacking copy that obscures the service offer. Jargon-first copy that a non-technical buyer has to decode is an anti-reference too: keep the terminal feeling, but the business message always outranks the texture. Around the AI offer, avoid AI-consultancy hype language (revolutionize, unlock, supercharge, 10x); state what gets built and what becomes dependable.

## Design Principles

1. Business value first: every surface states the outcome in plain language before the technical texture; visitors should understand the offer, fit, and next action before they decode the visual system.
2. Direct operator signal: keep Wojciech visible as the accountable specialist behind all four routes.
3. Warm precision: diagnostic and specific, but welcoming; the control room has a chair for the client.
4. Terminal identity with hierarchy: preserve the control-room feeling, but use it to organize decisions, not to prove technical depth.
5. Evidence over theater: use selected collaborations, RZETELNA credibility, structured process, and specific outputs instead of inflated claims or AI hype.
6. Contact without friction: email remains the fastest route; the form must never become the only way forward.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal accessibility target is required for now. Future interface work should still preserve readable contrast, keyboard-usable navigation, clear form states, visible focus, useful fallback copy, and reduced-motion alternatives for boot, scan, sweep, pulse, and scramble effects.
