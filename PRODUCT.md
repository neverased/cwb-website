# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Companies, founders, product leaders, and technical teams looking for a direct outside operator for technical consulting and delivery. They arrive with practical concerns: fragile delivery, unclear technical direction, media-heavy complexity, noisy product work, weak architecture, or a need for independent review before cost and risk grow.

The strongest-fit visitor is not browsing for a generic agency. They have a concrete problem, limited patience for sales theater, and need to understand quickly whether Wojciech can inspect the situation, reduce ambiguity, and help move the work forward.

## Product Purpose

The site presents Wojciech Bajer's one-person company as a selective consulting and delivery practice across multimedia systems, software engineering, application architecture, and company, product, or technology audits.

Success means a qualified visitor quickly understands four things:

1. What problems Wojciech can take on.
2. How the work moves from diagnosis to useful output.
3. Why direct one-person accountability is a trust signal, not a limitation.
4. How to route a concrete issue into contact without unnecessary friction.

The primary conversion path is direct email. The protected contact form exists as a secondary route, with email still visible when the form is loading or unavailable.

## Brand Personality

Technical, direct, diagnostic.

The brand should feel like a focused operator opening a live control surface, not a polished agency brochure. It should signal calm technical command, business usefulness, and direct accountability. The voice should be specific, plain, and evidence-led: diagnose the problem, expose the constraint, choose the route, leave durable artifacts.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic agency polish, bloated SaaS landing-page patterns, vague innovation language, decorative case-study theater, and portfolio design that feels more interested in presentation than problem solving.

Do not make the site feel like a large anonymous consultancy. Do not bury the operator behind abstract company language. Do not turn technical identity into costume hacking copy that obscures the service offer. Keep the terminal feeling, but make the hierarchy clear enough for non-technical clients to understand the business value.

## Design Principles

1. Clarity first: visitors should understand the offer, fit, and next action before they decode the visual system.
2. Direct operator signal: keep Wojciech visible as the accountable specialist behind the work.
3. Business value through technical clarity: translate media, software, architecture, and audit work into risk reduction, cleaner delivery, and durable handoff.
4. Terminal identity with hierarchy: preserve the control-room feeling, but use it to organize decisions, not to hide the message.
5. Evidence over theater: use selected collaborations, RZETELNA credibility, structured process, and specific outputs instead of inflated claims.
6. Contact without friction: email remains the fastest route; the form must never become the only way forward.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal accessibility target is required for now. Future interface work should still preserve readable contrast, keyboard-usable navigation, clear form states, visible focus, useful fallback copy, and reduced-motion alternatives for boot, scan, pulse, and scramble effects.
