# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Companies, founders, product leaders, and technical teams looking for a direct outside operator for IT services. They arrive with practical concerns: fragile delivery, unclear technical direction, media-heavy complexity, product noise, or a need for independent review before cost and risk grow.

## Product Purpose

The site presents Wojciech Bajer's one-person company as a selective consulting and delivery practice across multimedia systems, software engineering, application architecture, and product or technology audits. Success means a qualified visitor quickly understands the work, trusts the operating style, and can route a concrete problem into direct contact.

## Brand Personality

Technical, direct, diagnostic. The brand should read as a problem solver that reduces noise, addresses business concerns directly, and turns technical clarity into real business value.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic agency polish, bloated SaaS landing-page patterns, vague innovation language, decorative case-study theater, and portfolio design that feels more interested in presentation than problem solving. Do not make the site feel like a large anonymous consultancy; the one-person operator model is part of the trust signal.

## Design Principles

1. Show operational clarity before visual flourish.
2. Keep the one-person expert signal visible: direct voice, direct routes, direct accountability.
3. Connect technical decisions to business value, not only implementation detail.
4. Treat media, software, architecture, and audits as related problem-solving surfaces.
5. Preserve the existing terminal-grade identity when improving the interface.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal accessibility target is required for now. Future interface work should still preserve readable contrast, keyboard-usable navigation, clear form states, and reduced-motion fallbacks for terminal and scramble effects.
